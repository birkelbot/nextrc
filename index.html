Be a Creator, Designer,n Innovator

  • No Age/School Restrictions

    Compete even as a college student or post-academic!

  • August Kickoff | November Competition

    Most educational robotics programs have their build season in the spring. NEXT Robotics Competition features a fall build season to avoid overlap and allow competitors to continue as mentors in the spring.

  • Midwest Venue

    Initially, NEXT Robotics Competition will have just one event in the Midwest. In the future, NEXT will look to expand to new locations based on interest.

The Game

  • 60-lb Robots

    The weight limit was chosen with cost in mind. A sample chassis design will be made available to demonstrate low cost and technical barrier-to-entry.

  • Custom Electronics

    Teams can use any communcications and controls platform they want, and even some high-power brushless motors will be allowed*.

  • Low Cost

    By design, teams could compete with a budget as little as $500, which will be demonstrated by the sample chassis design. The majority of components are also reusable.

  • Alliances

    Learning from the success of other educational robotics programs, NEXT Robotics Competition will feature alliances of robots working together to accomplish the game challenge.

  • "We are what we celebrate" - Dean Kamen

    Keep celebrating! NEXT Robotics Competition adds a "pro" level beyond educational robotics programs, giving students visible role-model competitors to aspire to.

  • Build a World of Creators

    With the rapid emergence of automation, our world is desperately in need of a cultural shift towards creativity. NEXT Robotics Competition is a sport that enables people to practice their creative skills, which will lead to a productive, sustainable society.

  • Hone Teamwork and Communication Skills

    Sports are commonly regarded as a valuable activity to build teamwork and communication skills. NEXT Robotics Competition is a sport and provides the same benefits to those with interests outside of traditional sports.

  • Adults Like to Compete, Too!

    NEXT Robotics Competition provides an outlet for adult designers to put their own skills to the test outside of the educational robotics spectrum of K-12 robotics programs. And because of the timing of the build seasons, NEXT competitors can still be adult mentors in many educational robotics programs.
